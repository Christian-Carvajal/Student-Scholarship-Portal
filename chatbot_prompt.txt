KNOWLEDGE BASE

1. Basic Qualification Criteria

Target Applicants:
Filipino citizens currently enrolled in or have completed senior high school or college.

Academic Requirements:
Strong academic performance, typically a General Weighted Average (GWA) of 80% to 93%, depending on the specific scholarship.

Financial Need:
Income limits apply to ensure assistance goes to deserving students.

Specific Groups:
Some scholarships target STEM students, coconut farmer families, female students, or vocational trainees.

Partner Organizations:
CHED, DOST, TESDA, SM Foundation, Ayala Foundation, PCSO.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Available Scholarship Programs

SM College Scholarship (SM Foundation)
Amount: ~₱80,000 - ₱120,000 per year.

Details:
For incoming college students with strong academics and financial need.
Covers full tuition, monthly allowance, leadership training, and possible SM employment after graduation.

Megaworld Foundation Scholarship
Amount: ~₱70,000 - ₱120,000 per year.

Details:
For students in business, IT, and engineering.
Covers full or partial tuition, allowance, and career opportunities within Megaworld companies.

Ayala U-Go Scholarship (Ayala Foundation)
Amount: ~₱60,000 - ₱120,000 per year.

Details:
Focuses on female students with strong academic potential and financial need.
Provides tuition support, living allowance, and mentorship.

Aboitiz Scholarship Program (Aboitiz Foundation)
Amount: ~₱60,000 - ₱100,000 per year.

Details:
For engineering, business, and IT students.
Includes tuition assistance, leadership training, and work opportunities within Aboitiz companies.

--------------------------------------------------

3. How to Apply (4 Steps)

Step 1: Create or Login Account
Register using your Student ID or sign in with existing credentials.

Step 2: Available Scholarship Programs
Browse available scholarships, use search or GWA filters, and click Apply on your chosen program.

Step 3: Complete the Application Form
Fill out personal information, academic details, contact information, program, year level, and your letter of intent.

Step 4: Upload All Required Documents
Submit identity, academic, enrollment, income, character, and photo documents before final submission.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Required Documents

Note:
Prepare TWO (2) sets of documents.

Set 1:
Authenticated or Certified True Copies and photocopies for submission.

Set 2:
Original copies for office verification.

For Renewing Applicants:
1. Enrollment Proof (PDF of Certificate of Enrollment or Registration Form)
2. Academic Proof (PDF of latest Form 138 or Transcript of Records)
3. Proof of Identity (Government-issued ID or PSA Birth Certificate)

For New Applicants:
1. Enrollment Proof
2. Academic Proof
3. Proof of Identity
4. Proof of Income (ITR, Tax Exemption, or Certificate of Indigency)
5. Character Reference (Certificate of Good Moral)
6. Recent Photo (2x2 ID picture)

List of Acceptable IDs:
School ID  
Passport  
National ID  
Driver's License  
Voter's ID  
PhilHealth ID  
Postal ID  
Senior Citizen ID / PWD ID

If Applicable (Additional Documents):
- Income documents for all earning household members
- Updated character reference
- Recent photo with white background and name label
- Enrollment load summary
- Academic standing summary
- Identity revalidation

--------------------------------------------------

5. Maintaining Scholarship Status

Core Requirements:
- Submit renewal forms on schedule.
- Maintain passing academic standing (no failing, incomplete, or dropped subjects).
- Follow scholarship provider guidelines.

Penalties for Offenses:

1st Offense:
Probation or Warning

2nd Offense:
Suspension from the scholarship for the semester

3rd Offense:
Termination from the scholarship
